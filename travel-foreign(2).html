China

China - where ancient wisdom and modern ambition intertwine. For 5000 years, this civilization has evolved yet remained rooted in its philosophical heritage. From Confucian ideals to cutting-edge innovation, from tranquil temples to buzzing megacities, China embodies constant transformation. The Great Wall stands witness to history while high-speed trains race toward the future. A land of pandas and poets, of tea ceremonies and tech giants, China's story continues to unfold, blending tradition with relentless progress in its unique dance through time.

Summer Palace, Beijing

The most well-preserved royal palace and imperial garden in China

Terracotta Army, Xi'an

The burial pits accompanying the Mausoleum of Qin Shi Huang, is often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World."

The Forbidden City, Beijing

The largest and best-preserved wooden architectural complex in the world

Potala Palace, Lhasa

The highest and most magnificent palace in the world.

Longmen Grottoes, Luoyang

One of the Three Great Buddhist Grottoes in China

The Great Wall, Beijing

One of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a representative masterpiece of ancient Chinese military defense engineering

Zhangjiajie National Park, Hunan

UNESCO World Natural Heritage Site famous for its stone pillar forest landscape

Jiuzhaigou Valley, Sichuan

Renowned as a “fairyland on earth”, famous for its colorful lakes, waterfalls, and stunning natural scenery.

The Li River, Guangxi

Famous for its karst landscape, featuring stunning limestone peaks along its banks.

Yellow Mountain (Huangshan), Anhui

Known as the “Number One Wonder Mountain in China”

Mount Everest Base Camp, Tibet

Starting point for climbers on the northern route to the world’s highest peak, Mount Everest (8848.86m).

Yuanyang Terraces, Yunnan

One of the most spectacular rice terrace landscapes in the world.

Lijiang Old Town, Yunnan

Well-preserved ancient city of ethnic minorities and a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage Site.

Suzhou Gardens, Jiangsu

Masterpiece of Chinese private gardens and a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage Site.

Pingyao Ancient City, Shanxi

Outstanding example of a Han Chinese city from the Ming and Qing dynasties.

The Bund, Shanghai

Historic waterfront area and one of China’s most famous landmarks.

Wuzhen Water Town,Zhejiang

Typical ancient water town and is often referred to as “China’s last riverine household”

Chengdu Panda Base, Sichuan

One of the world’s leading conservation centers for giant pandas.

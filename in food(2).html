International Food

International cuisine has a long history, deeply influenced by various cultures around the world. Blending spices,

culinary techniques, and traditions, dishes like sushi, pasta, and tacos embody the global heritage.

Food has become an inseparable part of cultural identity, reflecting the rich diversity of different nations.

SUSHI

Sushi originated in Japan as a traditional dish that dates back to the 8th century. It was initially developed as a method of preserving fish by fermenting it with rice. Over time, the fermentation process was shortened, leading to the creation of vinegar-seasoned rice, which became the foundation of modern sushi. Originally, sushi was a simple dish with fresh fish and rice, but as it evolved, different varieties such as nigiri, maki, and sashimi emerged.

INDIA CURRY

Curry originated in India thousands of years ago, made with a blend of spices like turmeric, cumin, and chili. Initially vegetarian, it later evolved to include meats like chicken and lamb. As Indian cuisine spread globally, curry influenced dishes in many countries. Today, it remains a beloved dish in various forms, such as butter chicken and masala.

PAD THAI

Pad Thai originated in Thailand as a popular street food. It was introduced in the 1930s to promote nationalism and rice noodle consumption. Made with stir-fried rice noodles, shrimp or chicken, eggs, and peanuts, it quickly became a global favorite. Today, Pad Thai is a signature dish of Thai cuisine, enjoyed worldwide.

CHINESE HOT POT

Chinese Hot Pot dates back over 1,000 years, originating from the Mongolian steppes. It features a simmering broth where diners cook meat, seafood, and vegetables at the table. Over time, regional variations developed, such as spicy Sichuan hot pot. Today, it is a beloved social dining experience across China and beyond.

Learn how to cook it




PIZZA

Pizza originated in Italy, dating back to ancient flatbreads with toppings. The modern pizza, with tomato sauce and cheese, emerged in Naples in the 18th century. Originally a humble street food, it gained worldwide popularity, evolving into various styles like Neapolitan, New York, and deep-dish. Today, pizza is a global culinary icon.

Learn how to cook it




SHAWARMA

Shawarma originated in the Middle East, inspired by the Ottoman-era doner kebab. Made by stacking marinated meat on a vertical spit, it is slowly roasted and shaved into wraps or platters. Traditionally served with pita, garlic sauce, and pickles, shawarma has become a favorite street food across the world.

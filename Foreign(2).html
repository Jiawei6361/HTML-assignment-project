Festival around the world!

South Korea

In South Korea, Chuseok is the harvest festival, celebrated on the 15th day of the 8th month of the lunar calendar, similar to the Chinese Mid-Autumn Festival. During Chuseok, families gather to pay respects to their ancestors through a ritual called Charye, offering food and prayers. People also enjoy traditional foods like songpyeon (rice cakes filled with sweet fillings) and makgeolli (rice wine). The festival is also a time for family reunions, games, and performances of traditional Korean music and dance. It's a celebration of harvest, gratitude, and family bonding.

Bangladesh

In Bangladesh, Pohela Boishakh is the celebration of the Bengali New Year, typically observed on April 14th. The day is marked by vibrant parades, traditional Bengali foods, and wearing colorful clothes. People visit family and friends, exchange sweets, and enjoy performances of traditional music and dance. Markets and streets are decorated, and the day is filled with joyous celebrations to welcome the new year with hope and prosperity. It's a day of cultural pride, as well as a time for renewal and family bonding.

Thailand

In Thailand, Songkran, the Thai New Year, is celebrated from April 13th to 15th. Known for its famous water fights, Songkran is a time to wash away bad luck and start fresh for the new year. People throw water at each other in the streets, use water to clean Buddha statues, and visit temples to offer prayers and blessings. It's a joyful and family-oriented holiday, with people returning home to be with their loved ones, and it's filled with vibrant parades, traditional dances, and delicious food. The water symbolizes purity, renewal, and a fresh start.
